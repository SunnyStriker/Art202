Edible Cookie Dough Recipe

Let's get cooking!

Top recipe FAQ

What you'll need

  • All-purpose flour: heat treated to kill bacteria
  • Granulated sugar and brown sugar: for classic flavor
  • Unsalted butter: salted butter works too, but use a pinch of salt if used
  • Salt: for flavor
  • Vanilla extract: real vanilla for best flavor
  • Milk: so the cookie dough isn't dry
  • Mini or regular chocolate chips: whichever you prefer

How to make it

  • Heat treat the flour: Spread 3 cups in an 18 by 13 inch baking sheet. Bake in a preheated oven for 7 minutes, or until flour temperature is 160 degrees. Let it cool completely then transfer to an airtight container.
  • Butter, sugar, and salt: Add butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar to a mixing bowl, then sprinkle salt. With an electric mixer, mix until it is pale and fluffy.
  • Blend in liquids: Add in milk and vanilla, mix until well blended.
  • Blend in flour:Add in the heat treated flour, and mix until combined.
  • Chocolate: add chocolate chips, then fold with a rubber spatula until evenly distributed.

FAQ

  • How long does cookie dough last in the fridge? Serve right away, or save in the fridge in a container for up to a week.
  • Can I freeze it? Yes, frozen cookie dough can be stored up to 2 months.
  • Can I add it to ice cream? Yes, if you don't add a lot of milk, you can shape the dough easily.
  • Can I mix by hand? Yes, you can mix by hand. Melting the butter is recommended to make the mixing easier in this case.
  • Can I bake this cookie dough? No, due to the lack of eggs and baking soda, the cookies will be flat if cooked.
